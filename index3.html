Release of WAEC Results

Announcement of WAEC Exam Results

The West African Examinations Council, WAEC, has published the outcomes of the 2023 West African Senior School Certificate Examinations (WASSCE).

The Nigeria National Office of the West African Examinations Council (WAEC), Mr. Patrick Areghan, revealed the release of the results on Monday during a press briefing at its headquarters in Yaba, Lagos State.

The examination organization mentioned that eight states, which owe the West African Examinations Council for various services rendered, will not have the WASSCE results for their candidates in 2023 released.

Around 1.6 million candidates participated in the exam.

Patrick stated that the results of the 2023 West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE) can now be accessed along with the candidates' certificates.

WAEC expressed concerns about the challenges posed by the inability of some states to pay for services rendered.

The examination body indicated that out of the total of 1,613,733 candidates who sat for the exam, the results of 262,803 candidates are being withheld due to reports of examination malpractice.

Mr. Patrick announced an improved pass rate, with 1,361,608 candidates (84.38%) obtaining credit or higher in at least five subjects, with or without English Language or Mathematics.

Additionally, 1,287,920 candidates (79.81%) obtained credits or higher in a minimum of five subjects, including English Language and Mathematics.

He mentioned, "I'm pleased to inform that candidates who took the 2023 WASSCE and have no outstanding issues can access their results, including their certificates."

"About eight states have outstanding payments to WAEC. We won't name them all as some are in the process of making payments.

However, Zamfara and Niger states have the highest outstanding debts. Furthermore, Zamfara did not present any candidates for this year's WASSCE."

A total of 1,621,853 SS3 students from 20,851 secondary schools registered for the WASSCE. Among them, 798,810 were males, representing 49.25%, while 823,043 were females, constituting 50.75% of the total candidature.

According to WAEC, the statistics show a further change in the number of females and males compared to the 2022 exam.

How to Access 2023 WASSCE Results Online

You don't need to buy any scratch card or PIN to view your result. Candidates should check the Smart Identity Card used during the exam for their Result Checker PIN and Serial Number.

Follow the steps below:

  1. Visit the WAEC 2023 result checking portal at https://www.waecdirect.org.
  2. Enter your WAEC Examination Number in the provided field.
  3. Select the Examination Year (2023).
  4. Select the Examination Type (SCHOOL CANDIDATE RESULT).
  5. Enter the Card Serial Number.
  6. Enter the Card PIN.
  7. Finally, click the "Submit" button to access your 2023 WAEC Result.

How to Check WASSCE Results via SMS

Send an SMS in the following format:

WAEC*ExamNo*PIN*ExamYear

To the short-code 32327 (MTN, Airtel & Glo subscribers)

For example: WAEC*4250101001*123456789012*2022 to 32327

Note: Make sure to adhere strictly to the provided format. The message should not contain any spaces.

Wait for your result to be sent to your phone via SMS. (SMS Cost N30 Only)

If you encounter issues while checking your result

Please send the following details to waecsupport@fleettechltd.com for verification and assistance:

  1. Error message displayed.
  2. Your examination number.
  3. Type of Exam.
  4. Year of Exam.
